Bypass Surgery Cost in India 2025 — Complete Guide for International Patients

📅 Published: April 2025 ✍️ By: MedYatra Editorial Team ⏱️ 9 min read 👁️ Reviewed for medical accuracy

If you or a family member has been told you need bypass surgery — formally called Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG) — and you are looking at the cost of the procedure in your country, India is very likely the most important alternative you haven't yet considered.

Bypass surgery in India costs $4,500 to $7,000 at top internationally accredited hospitals. In the United States, the same surgery costs $80,000 to $120,000. In the United Kingdom, the NHS has waiting lists of 6–18 months. In many Middle Eastern and African countries, the procedure is either unavailable or performed at a fraction of the quality.

Key fact: India performs more cardiac surgeries per year than almost any other country in the world. Hospitals like Narayana Health perform over 300 bypass surgeries per month — meaning surgeons have extraordinary experience levels. Practice volume is one of the strongest predictors of surgical outcomes.

Is Bypass Surgery Safe in India?

This is the question every patient asks first, and it deserves a direct answer: yes, bypass surgery is very safe at India's top hospitals.

Narayana Health publishes its cardiac surgery mortality data publicly. Its CABG mortality rate is under 1% for elective procedures — comparable to the best US centres. The hospital's founder, Dr. Devi Shetty, trained at Guy's Hospital in London and has been widely cited in global health economics research for delivering world-class outcomes at a fraction of Western cost.

India's cardiac surgery safety record is strong for several structural reasons:

  • Volume: Indian cardiac surgeons perform far more surgeries per year than most Western counterparts. Surgical skill is closely tied to repetition.
  • Training: Many senior surgeons at Apollo, Medanta and Escorts trained at NHS, Mayo Clinic or Johns Hopkins before returning to India.
  • Equipment: JCI-accredited hospitals use the same equipment as Western hospitals — Medtronic heart-lung machines, Zimmer retractors, imported sutures.
  • Accreditation: JCI (Joint Commission International) holds hospitals to the same standards as US hospitals. NABH is the Indian equivalent of the UK's CQC inspection system.

Medical Visa for India — Bypass Surgery

International patients travelling to India for surgery require an Indian Medical e-Visa (e-Medvisa). This is separate from a regular tourist visa and is applied for online. Key requirements:

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months remaining validity)
  • Hospital invitation letter from a recognised Indian hospital
  • Proof of medical condition (angiogram report, doctor's letter)
  • Recent passport-sized photograph
  • Proof of travel insurance
  • Return flight booking or itinerary

The medical e-Visa allows a stay of up to 60 days and is typically approved within 3–5 business days. The fee is approximately $25 USD depending on nationality. MedYatra's Premium Plan includes full preparation of the hospital invitation letter required for this application.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does bypass surgery cost in India in 2025?

Bypass surgery in India costs $4,500–$7,000 at top private hospitals, all-inclusive. Government hospitals like AIIMS Delhi charge $2,000–$3,500. These prices include surgery, ICU, ward stay, medicines and post-op physiotherapy.

Which is the best hospital for bypass surgery in India?

Escorts Heart Institute (Delhi), Apollo Hospitals (Chennai), Narayana Health (Bengaluru), Medanta (Gurugram) and AIIMS Delhi are consistently ranked the best for cardiac surgery in India for international patients.

How long will I need to stay in India?

Plan for a minimum 3-week stay: 10–14 days in hospital and 7 days of nearby recovery before flying home. Long-haul flights too soon after open-heart surgery carry risks — most surgeons recommend waiting 3–4 weeks minimum.

Is bypass surgery done differently in India?

No. The surgical technique, equipment and protocols are identical to those used in the USA and UK. India uses the same imported Medtronic and Boston Scientific equipment, the same Zimmer retractors, and the same post-op recovery protocols as Western cardiac centres.

Can I bring a companion with me?

Yes. An attendant visa can be arranged alongside your medical visa for one companion. Most hospitals allow one attendant to stay in the room. MedYatra helps arrange accommodation for companions near the hospital during the ICU period.
