About MedYatra

India's trusted medical tourism guide — built to remove confusion, cost and distance from international healthcare.

Why MedYatra Exists

Every year, more than 1.4 million people from across the world travel to India for medical treatment. Some come because their own country cannot offer the surgery they need. Others come because the same procedure in the USA, UK or Europe costs 5–10 times more. Many come because they have waited months on a public healthcare waitlist while their condition worsens.

India has some of the finest hospitals, surgeons and medical technology in the world. Apollo Hospitals, Tata Memorial, Narayana Health, AIIMS and Medanta are internationally accredited institutions that rival anything in the West — at a fraction of the cost. Yet most international patients arrive in India without guidance, without knowing which hospital is right for their specific condition, and without the language or logistical support that makes a medical trip survivable rather than stressful.

"MedYatra was created because we believe no patient should face a life-saving medical decision alone — especially in a foreign country. Our job is to remove every barrier between a patient and the best care India can offer."

MedYatra was founded in 2024 in New Delhi with a simple mission: to be the most trustworthy, most transparent, and most practically useful guide for international patients seeking medical treatment in India. We do not take commissions from hospitals. We do not push patients toward expensive private options they do not need. We match patients to the right hospital for their condition, budget and location — and then walk alongside them every step of the way.

What We Know — Our Expertise

MedYatra specialises in connecting international patients with Indian hospitals across the following medical domains. Our guides, hospital assessments and cost data are researched directly from hospital sources, government health ministry data and patient outcome reports.

  • Cardiac Surgery — Bypass (CABG), valve replacement, angioplasty and stenting. India performs more than 100,000 cardiac surgeries per year on international patients.
  • Oncology — Breast, lung, colorectal, blood, cervical and prostate cancer. Surgery, chemotherapy, radiation, immunotherapy and targeted therapy.
  • Orthopaedics — Total knee replacement, hip replacement, spinal fusion, disc surgery and sports injuries. Robot-assisted surgery available at leading centres.
  • Neurology & Neurosurgery — Brain tumour surgery, epilepsy management, stroke rehabilitation, Gamma Knife and CyberKnife radiosurgery.
  • Organ Transplants — Kidney, liver and bone marrow transplants. India's success rates match top European programmes.
  • Reproductive Medicine — IVF, IUI, ICSI, egg freezing and surrogacy facilitation (where legally applicable).
  • Ophthalmology — Cataract surgery, LASIK, retinal detachment repair and glaucoma treatment.
  • Medical Visa & Travel Logistics — e-Medvisa application support, airport transfers, hospital-adjacent accommodation and interpreter services.

How We Are Different

Most medical tourism agencies earn commission from hospitals — which means they are incentivised to send you to the most expensive option, not the best one. MedYatra operates on a flat patient fee model. Our $40 Premium Plan is paid by the patient, not the hospital. This means every recommendation we make is genuinely in your interest.

We also publish all our hospital and cost data publicly — for free. You do not need to pay anything to access our treatment guides, hospital rankings or cost comparisons. The $40 plan is for patients who want hands-on coordination support for their actual trip.
