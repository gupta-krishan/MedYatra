Bypass Surgery Cost in India 2025 — $4,500 vs $90,000 in USA

Full cost breakdown, top hospitals ranked, visa process and everything an international patient needs to know before travelling to India for bypass surgery.

📅 Updated: April 2025 ✍️ MedYatra Editorial ⏱️ 9 min read

Bypass surgery — formally called Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG) — is one of the most common reasons international patients travel to India. The numbers are stark: the same procedure that costs $80,000–$120,000 in the United States costs $4,500–$7,000 in India at a JCI-accredited hospital — using the same Medtronic equipment, with surgeons who may have trained at Johns Hopkins or Guy's Hospital in London.

Bottom line: Bypass surgery in India costs $4,500–$7,000 all-inclusive. That is a saving of up to $115,000 compared to the USA — enough to cover flights, hotel, food and full concierge support many times over.

Is Bypass Surgery Safe in India?

Yes — at the hospitals listed above. The data is publicly available. Narayana Health publishes its cardiac surgery mortality rate: under 1% for elective CABG. This is comparable to Cleveland Clinic and Mayo Clinic. The reasons cardiac surgery is safe at India's top hospitals:

  • High volume: Narayana Health performs 300+ bypass surgeries per month. Volume is the strongest predictor of surgical outcomes — more practice means fewer errors.
  • Imported equipment: Medtronic heart-lung machines, the same used in the USA.
  • Internationally trained surgeons: Many senior cardiac surgeons at Apollo and Escorts trained in the USA or UK before returning to India.
  • JCI accreditation: The Joint Commission International holds hospitals to identical standards as American hospitals.

FAQ — Bypass Surgery in India

How much does bypass surgery cost in India in 2025?

$4,500–$7,000 all-inclusive at top private hospitals. AIIMS Delhi (government) costs $2,000–$3,500 but has longer waiting periods for international patients.

How long will I stay in India for bypass surgery?

Plan for 3–4 weeks total: 10–14 days in hospital and 7–10 days of nearby recovery before flying home. Most surgeons advise against long-haul flights for at least 3 weeks after open-heart surgery.

What reports do I need to bring for bypass surgery in India?

Angiogram report (essential), ECG, echocardiogram report, recent blood tests (CBC, kidney function, blood sugar), list of current medications, and any previous cardiac surgery reports.
