Medical Tourism in India 2025 — Complete Guide for International Patients

Everything you need to know before travelling to India for medical treatment — hospitals, costs, visa, safety and what to expect.

📅 Updated: April 2025 ✍️ MedYatra Editorial ⏱️ 12 min read ✅ Medically reviewed

India has become the world's most sought-after destination for affordable, high-quality medical treatment. In 2024, over 1.4 million international patients travelled to India for surgery, cancer treatment, fertility care, organ transplants and more — from 68 countries. The primary reason is simple: the same procedure that costs $90,000 in the United States costs $5,000 in India, performed by a surgeon who may have trained at Johns Hopkins or the NHS.

Key fact: Bypass surgery costs $4,500–$7,000 in India at a JCI-accredited hospital vs $80,000–$120,000 in the USA — a saving of over $75,000 for a single procedure.

Why Do Patients Choose India for Medical Treatment?

Medical tourism in India has grown at 15–20% per year for the past decade. Patients choose India for five main reasons:

  • Cost: 60–80% lower than USA, UK or Europe for identical procedures
  • Quality: 340+ hospitals hold JCI or NABH accreditation — the same standard as American hospitals
  • No waiting lists: Specialist appointments within 24–48 hours, surgery within days
  • English-speaking doctors: India produces more English-speaking doctors than any other country
  • International training: Senior surgeons at top hospitals trained at Mayo Clinic, Johns Hopkins, Guy's Hospital and other leading institutions

Best Hospitals in India for International Patients

Not all hospitals in India are equal. For international patients, only JCI or NABH accredited hospitals should be considered. Here are India's top hospitals by specialty:

Best Cardiac Surgery Hospitals in India

  • Escorts Heart Institute, New Delhi — Performs 5,000+ bypass surgeries per year. India's first dedicated cardiac hospital. Cost: $4,500–$6,500 for bypass.
  • Apollo Hospitals, Chennai — JCI-accredited. Surgeons trained at Texas Heart Institute and Johns Hopkins. Cost: $5,000–$7,000 for bypass.
  • Narayana Health, Bengaluru — World's highest cardiac surgery volume. Mortality rate under 1% for elective bypass — published publicly. Cost: $4,500–$6,000.
  • Medanta The Medicity, Gurugram — Led by Dr. Naresh Trehan, trained at NYU. India's largest private cardiac programme. Cost: $5,500–$7,000.

Best Cancer Hospitals in India

  • Tata Memorial Centre, Mumbai — WHO Collaborating Centre. India's premier cancer hospital. Government rates are lowest in India.
  • HCG Cancer Centre, Bengaluru — India's largest cancer hospital network. 30+ centres across India.
  • Apollo Cancer Centre, Chennai — Has proton therapy, one of few in Asia. JCI-accredited.

Best Orthopaedic Hospitals in India

  • Apollo Hospitals, New Delhi — MAKO robotic knee replacement. JCI-accredited. Cost: $5,000–$8,500.
  • Medanta The Medicity, Gurugram — Full orthopaedic programme. Cost: $5,500–$8,000.
  • Kokilaben Dhirubhai Ambani Hospital, Mumbai — JCI-accredited. Cost: $5,000–$7,500.

Medical Visa for India — Complete Process

International patients travelling to India for medical treatment require an Indian Medical e-Visa (e-Medvisa), not a regular tourist visa. The process:

  1. Apply online at indianvisaonline.gov.in — select "Medical" as the visa category
  2. Documents needed: Passport (6+ months validity), hospital invitation letter, medical diagnosis documents, passport photo, return flight details
  3. Hospital invitation letter: Your chosen hospital provides this. MedYatra prepares it for Premium Plan members.
  4. Processing time: 3–5 business days. Fee: approximately $25 depending on nationality.
  5. Validity: 60 days. Can be extended at the Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO) for ongoing treatment.

Important: Never travel to India on a tourist visa for medical treatment. If detected, you may be denied treatment and face visa complications. Always apply for the Medical e-Visa.

Is Medical Tourism in India Safe?

The honest answer is: yes, at accredited hospitals. Here is what the evidence shows:

  • JCI (Joint Commission International) accreditation applies identical standards to US hospital certification. India has 60+ JCI-accredited hospitals.
  • Narayana Health's published cardiac mortality data shows under 1% for elective bypass surgery — comparable to Mayo Clinic and Cleveland Clinic.
  • India uses the same imported surgical equipment as Western hospitals: Medtronic heart-lung machines, Zimmer Biomet joint implants, Boston Scientific stents.
  • Many senior surgeons at Apollo, Medanta and Escorts trained at NHS, Johns Hopkins, Mayo Clinic or other leading Western institutions before returning to India.
  • The risk is higher at non-accredited hospitals — always verify JCI or NABH status before choosing a hospital. MedYatra only recommends accredited hospitals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is medical treatment so cheap in India?

Medical treatment in India costs 70–80% less than in the USA or UK because of lower labour costs, different malpractice insurance structures, government-subsidised medical education, and India's status as the world's largest generic medicine manufacturer. The savings are not due to lower quality — India has 340+ JCI and NABH accredited hospitals using the same imported equipment as Western hospitals.

Is medical tourism in India safe?

Yes, at accredited hospitals. JCI-accredited hospitals in India meet the same standards as American hospitals. Surgeons at Apollo, Medanta and Narayana Health trained at Johns Hopkins, Mayo Clinic and NHS. Narayana Health publishes its cardiac surgery mortality data publicly — under 1% for elective bypass, comparable to the best US centres.

What is the best city in India for medical tourism?

The best cities for medical tourism in India are New Delhi (AIIMS, Fortis, Max, Escorts), Chennai (Apollo — best for cardiac), Mumbai (Tata Memorial for cancer, Kokilaben), Bengaluru (Narayana Health, Manipal, Cloudnine for IVF) and Gurugram (Medanta, Artemis). Delhi and Chennai are the most popular for international patients overall.

How long do I need to stay in India for treatment?

It depends on the procedure. For bypass surgery: 3–4 weeks total (14 days in hospital, 7–10 days recovery before flying). For knee replacement: 2–3 weeks. For IVF: 12–18 days per cycle. For kidney transplant: 3–5 weeks. Most doctors recommend waiting at least 3 weeks after major open surgery before long-haul flights.
