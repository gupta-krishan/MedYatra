Knee Replacement Cost in India 2025 — $5,000 vs $50,000 in USA

Full cost breakdown, implant brands, robot-assisted surgery options, top hospitals ranked and step-by-step guide for international patients.

📅 Updated: April 2025 ✍️ MedYatra Editorial ⏱️ 8 min read

Knee replacement surgery — total knee arthroplasty (TKA) — is the most sought-after orthopaedic procedure among international patients travelling to India. Over 200,000 international patients had knee replacement surgery in India in 2024. The cost advantage is dramatic: the same surgery with the same Zimmer Biomet or Stryker implants costs $5,000–$8,500 in India versus $35,000–$70,000 in the USA.

Key fact: The implants used in India are identical to those used in America — Zimmer Biomet Persona, Stryker Triathlon and Smith & Nephew Journey, all manufactured in the USA and imported to India. The cost difference is in hospital charges and surgical fees, not implant quality.

Which Knee Implants Are Used in India?

This is the question most patients ask first. The answer is reassuring: top hospitals in India use the same internationally manufactured implants as American and European hospitals:

  • Zimmer Biomet Persona — made in Warsaw, Indiana, USA. The most widely used knee implant system in India's top hospitals.
  • Stryker Triathlon — made in Kalamazoo, Michigan, USA.
  • Smith & Nephew Journey — UK/USA manufactured. Available at select centres.
  • DePuy Synthes (Johnson & Johnson) — available at Apollo and Fortis.

The implant accounts for $1,800–$3,500 of the total surgery cost in India. In the USA, the same implant is billed at $10,000–$20,000 — not because it costs more to make, but because of hospital markup practices in the American healthcare system.

Robot-Assisted Knee Replacement in India (MAKO Surgery)

Robotic knee replacement using the Stryker MAKO system is now available in India at Apollo Hospitals Delhi and a handful of other centres. The MAKO system creates a 3D model of your knee from CT scans and guides the surgeon to place the implant with sub-millimetre precision. Benefits:

  • More accurate implant positioning → better alignment → longer implant life
  • Less bone removed → smaller incision
  • Faster recovery — many patients walk the same day
  • Lower risk of revision surgery

Cost of MAKO robotic knee replacement in India: $6,500–$9,500, compared to $45,000–$80,000 in the USA for the same procedure.

FAQ — Knee Replacement in India

How much does knee replacement cost in India?

Total knee replacement costs $5,000–$8,500 per knee at top JCI-accredited private hospitals in India. This includes surgery, premium implants, 7 nights hospital stay, anaesthesia, medicines and in-hospital physiotherapy.

Are the knee implants in India the same as in the USA?

Yes. Top hospitals use imported Zimmer Biomet, Stryker and DePuy implants — the same brands manufactured in the USA and used in American hospitals. The difference in total cost is in hospital charges and surgeon fees, not implant quality.

How long do I need to stay in India after knee replacement?

Plan for 2–3 weeks total: 7 days in hospital and 7–10 days of physiotherapy and recovery nearby before flying home. Long-haul flights too soon carry a risk of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) — wait at least 14 days after hospital discharge.

Can I get both knees replaced at the same time in India?

Yes — bilateral (both knees) replacement is offered at top hospitals. Total cost for both knees: $9,000–$16,000 all-inclusive. Recovery is longer — plan for 3–4 weeks in India. This is commonly chosen by international patients since they are already making the journey.
